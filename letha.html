Letha

HTML/CSS/JS & Interaction Design

OBJECTIVE: Create a design and build a responsive website for the fictional shoe company Letha. Do market resarch to make the site appealing and functional for a group audience of choice.

View site

1. Process

My target audience of choice was men in the ages 20-30. I interviewed three friends in this age range, asking "open" questions (to not get simple "yes" or "no" answers) and asked them to complete simple tasks on three e-commerce sites for footwear.

I used the interviewes to make three personas to have a concrete target audience in mind while designing the site. I also used the information I gathered in my design choices; what the others sites did that worked well, and what did not work as well for my target audience.

Before coding, I did some quick pencil sketches of the overall layout of the site, and then I made wireframes for all the pages in Adobe XD for laptop, mobile and tablet.

2. Photos

Through my research I learned that photos made more impact on the target audience than graphic elements and illustrations. I did my best to find free high resolution photos with the correct mood and cohesive style. All photos were collected from Unsplash.com.

3. Design Principles

TYPOGRAPHY: I chose the sans serif font Roboto to use on the entire site. This is because I found during my market research that sans serif fonts are the most common used on these kinds of sites, and my users said they liked "a simple font like Helvetica" best. I think the font gives the site a clean and professional look, as well as the use of different weights breaks it up and helps with the typographic hierarchy.

Colors: I chose to go for black, white and grey to further play on the clean and professional look of the site. This makes the images attract the attention of the viewer, which is desireable according to my interviews.
